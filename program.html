GIVE A HELPING HAND
TO THOSE WHO NEED IT!


Helping poor people is a good thing to do. We can help by giving food, clothes, or toys that we don’t need anymore. We can also teach them new things, like reading or drawing. When we help, it makes them happy, and it makes us happy too. It’s like sharing a big, warm hug. It’s important to remember that everyone needs help sometimes, and it’s nice to be the one who gives that help. So, let’s always be ready to lend a hand to those who need it.
Helping poor people is a lovely act of kindness. When we help others, we feel happy inside. Imagine you have lots of toys, but your friend has none. You would feel sad for your friend, right? So, you might give them one of your toys. This is just like helping the poor. Some people don’t have food to eat, clothes to wear or a place to live. They may not have money to go to school or to see a doctor when they are sick. We can help them in many ways. We could give them food, clothes, or toys that we don’t need anymore. We could also help them learn new things, like reading and writing. We could spend time with them and make them feel loved. Our small acts of kindness can make a big difference in their lives. You see, when we help others, we also help ourselves. We learn to be kind, caring, and understanding. We become better people. So, let’s always remember to help the poor. It’s a nice thing to do and it makes our world a better place.

WE NEED VOLUNTEERS


Volunteering means giving your time and abilities to aid others (usually a nonprofit organization) without the want of financial compensation. Put simply, volunteering is a service to your community.

Many nonprofit organizations rely on volunteers to accomplish day-to-day objectives and to reach long-term goals. Miles4Migrants, for instance, relied solely on volunteer hours for their first few years of operation. Most nonprofits would not be able to do the important work they do without the power of volunteer work.

Nothing gets done by one person alone, which means we have to band together to make the changes we want to see in the world. Volunteerism has indeed been the fuel to the fires of change both on international and local fronts. Volunteering your time, skills, and resources is one of the most powerful ways to make a difference, to help others, and as it turns out, to enhance your own wellbeing.
